Grilled Sticky Ribs

Pork ribs that are cooked, sliced, and then glazed with sticky sauce.

The time required to follow this recipe is:

  • 15 minutes preparing
  • 2 hours and 15 minutes cooking
  • 2 hours and 30 minutes chilling

Ingredients

  • 4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 4 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 racks baby back pork ribs

Glaze

  • 1/3 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 250 degrees F (120 C).
  2. Mix salt, sugar, black peper, paprika, and garlic powder for dry rub inside a small bowl and set it aside.
  3. Line a large sheet pan with heavy-duty aluminum foil and place the ribs on the pan with bone side down.
  4. Poke with knife while making shallow slits across at an angle and repeat in opposite direction.
  5. Coat both sides with the dry rub prepared earlier. Flip the rib over to ensure bone side is facing downwards. Wrap tightly with tinfoil
  6. Transfer to center of preheated over and bake for 2 hours. Remove from oven and let it cool to room temperature.
  7. Refrigerate until cold and firm for at least 2 hours (or overnight).
  8. Preheat a charcoal grill until coals are very ashy and hot.
  9. Remove ribs from the refrigerator, unwrap, and brush off excess fat. Slice between the bones to separate each rib.
  10. Combine ketchup, brown sugar, soy sauce, vinegar, oil, and cayenne with a spoon in a bowl to make the glaze.
  11. Place rib in the grill and cook for 2 minutes. Flip and brush over glaze. Repeat glazing until satisified for about 10 minutes more.
  12. Remove and place on a plate. Apply one more application of glaze.
